# Nicomachean Ethics

The *Nicomachean Ethics* is [[aristotle]]'s best-known work on ethics, named after either his father or son, both named Nicomachus.

## Content

The work explores the nature of the good life and human flourishing (eudaimonia). Aristotle argues that happiness is the highest good and is achieved through the practice of virtue.

## Key Concepts

- **Eudaimonia**: Human flourishing as the ultimate goal
- **Virtue Ethics**: Character traits as the basis of ethics
- **The Golden Mean**: Virtue as the balance between extremes
- **Practical Wisdom**: The ability to discern right action
