# Guernica

_Guernica_ is a large oil painting by Pablo Picasso, created in response to the bombing of Guernica, a Basque town in northern Spain, by Nazi Germany and Fascist Italy.

## Description

The monumental painting (11.5 ft × 25.5 ft) depicts the suffering of people and animals in the aftermath of the bombing, rendered in stark black, white, and grey.

## Symbolism

- **Bull**: Spanish culture, brutality
- **Horse**: The suffering people
- **Light bulb**: The "eye of God" or modern technology
- **Broken sword**: Defeat of the people
- **Screaming figures**: Universal suffering

## History

Painted for the Spanish Republic's pavilion at the 1937 Paris International Exposition, Picasso refused to allow it to return to Spain until democracy was restored.

## Location

Now housed in the Museo Reina Sofía in Madrid, where it arrived in 1981 after the death of Franco.
