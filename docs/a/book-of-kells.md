# Book of Kells

The Book of Kells (Latin: *Codex Cenannensis*) is an illuminated manuscript Gospel book in Latin, created around 800 CE.

## Description

The manuscript contains the four Gospels of the New Testament, along with prefatory texts and tables. It is renowned for its lavish decoration and intricate artwork.

## Artistic Features

- Elaborate illuminated initials
- Interlaced patterns and knotwork
- Representations of humans, animals, and mythical beasts
- Vibrant colors including gold, purple, and lapis lazuli blue

## History

Believed to have been created at the monastery of Iona, Scotland, the book was later moved to Kells, Ireland. It is now kept at Trinity College Dublin.

## Significance

Considered one of Ireland's greatest national treasures and a masterwork of Western calligraphy and illumination.
