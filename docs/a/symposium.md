# Symposium

The *Symposium* (Greek: Συμπόσιον) is a philosophical text by [[plato]] dated c. 385–370 BCE.

## Content

The dialogue depicts a drinking party where guests give speeches in praise of Eros, the god of love. It includes Socrates' famous account of the teachings of Diotima.

## Key Speeches

- **Phaedrus**: Love as the oldest god
- **Pausanias**: Heavenly vs. common love
- **Aristophanes**: The myth of the split humans
- **Socrates/Diotima**: The ladder of love ascending to the Form of Beauty
