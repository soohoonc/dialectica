# Islamic Philosophy

**Time Period**: 800 - 1400

## Overview

The philosophical tradition within Islamic civilization integrating Greek philosophical methods with Islamic theology and jurisprudence

## Key Ideas

### [[individuals/al-kindi|Al-Kindi]]

- **Philosophy and Religion**: The harmonization of rational philosophy with revealed religion, establishing philosophy as compatible with Islamic faith
  - Tags: #IslamicPhilosophy, #ReasonAndRevelation, #Theology

### [[individuals/al-farabi|Al-Farabi]]

- **Perfect State**: The political philosophy describing an ideal state led by philosopher-prophets who combine philosophical wisdom with prophetic guidance
  - Tags: #IslamicPhilosophy, #PoliticalPhilosophy, #Prophecy

### [[individuals/avicenna|Avicenna]]

- **Distinction of Essence and Existence**: The metaphysical principle that in all things except God, essence and existence are distinct, with existence being added to essence
  - Tags: #IslamicPhilosophy, #Metaphysics, #Existence

### [[individuals/al-ghazali|Al-Ghazali]]

- **The Incoherence of Philosophers**: The critique of Aristotelian philosophy from the perspective of Islamic theology, particularly regarding causation and the eternity of the world
  - Tags: #IslamicPhilosophy, #Theology, #Critique

### [[individuals/averroes|Averroes]]

- **Double Truth Theory**: The philosophical position that philosophical and religious truths represent different levels of understanding the same reality
  - Tags: #IslamicPhilosophy, #ReasonAndRevelation, #Truth

### [[individuals/ibn-khaldun|Ibn Khaldun]]

- **Science of Society**: The systematic study of social phenomena, establishing the foundations of sociology and historical methodology
  - Tags: #IslamicPhilosophy, #Sociology, #HistoricalMethod

## Intellectual Networks

This tradition created sophisticated syntheses of Aristotelian, Platonic, and Stoic philosophy with Islamic theology, influencing both Islamic and Christian philosophical development.

### Most Influential Ideas

- The integration of philosophical reasoning with prophetic revelation
- Development of sophisticated metaphysical systems
- Advancement of Aristotelian logic and natural philosophy
- Creation of philosophical mysticism (Sufism)

## Context

Islamic philosophy emerged during the Abbasid period (750-1258) as Muslim scholars encountered Greek philosophical texts through translation movements in Baghdad, Cordoba, and other centers of Islamic learning. The tradition developed within the context of a rapidly expanding Islamic empire that encompassed diverse cultural and intellectual traditions.

The movement flourished in major centers like Baghdad, Cordoba, and Cairo, supported by caliphs and rulers who patronized learning. The tradition engaged with fundamental questions about the relationship between reason and revelation, the nature of God and creation, and the proper organization of society.

## Legacy

Islamic philosophy profoundly influenced world intellectual history:

- **[[periods/medieval-philosophy|Medieval Philosophy]]**: Transmitted Aristotelian philosophy to Christian Europe through translations and commentaries
- **Jewish Philosophy**: Influenced [[individuals/maimonides|Maimonides]] and other Jewish philosophers
- **Renaissance**: Islamic commentaries on Aristotle influenced Renaissance thought
- **Modern Islamic Thought**: Contemporary Muslim philosophers draw on classical Islamic philosophical resources
- **Comparative Philosophy**: Provides models for integrating philosophical reasoning with religious commitment

The tradition's sophisticated treatment of reason and revelation, its development of metaphysical systems, and its contribution to logic, ethics, and political philosophy continue to influence contemporary discussions of religious philosophy and the relationship between faith and reason.
