# Athens

Athens is the capital city of Greece and one of the most historically significant cities in Western civilization. In antiquity, it was the center of ancient Greek philosophy and learning.

## Philosophical Significance

Athens was home to:

- **The Academy**: Founded by [[plato]] around 387 BCE, considered the first institution of higher learning in the Western world
- **The Lyceum**: Founded by [[aristotle]] in 335 BCE, center of the Peripatetic school
- **The Stoa Poikile**: Meeting place of the Stoic philosophers
- **The Garden of Epicurus**: Center of Epicurean philosophy

## Golden Age

During the [[classical-greece|Classical period]], Athens achieved unprecedented cultural and intellectual heights under the leadership of Pericles. The city became the school of Hellas, attracting thinkers and artists from across the Greek world.

## Key Sites

- **The Parthenon**: Temple dedicated to Athena on the Acropolis
- **The Agora**: Public gathering space and marketplace, center of Athenian public life
- **The Pnyx**: Hill where the Athenian assembly met

## Figures Associated with Athens

- [[plato]]
- [[aristotle]]
- Socrates
- Pericles
- Solon

## Legacy

The philosophical traditions born in Athens—[[virtue-ethics]], Platonic idealism, Aristotelian logic—continue to shape Western thought today.
